# k-023 — Card priorities and prioritisation

**Done.** Cards now carry a priority, you can set it, see it at a glance, and each column automatically orders itself so the most important work sits at the top.

## What was added

**Four priority levels** — High, Medium, Low, and None (the default). You set priority in the card editor: open any card (or "+ New card") and there's a new **Priority** control next to Assignee, styled the same way as the Me/Claude toggle. High is red, Medium amber, Low grey.

**Visual cues on the card.** A card with a priority shows a small flag badge (⚑ High / ⚑ Medium / ⚑ Low) and a coloured stripe down its left edge, so you can scan a column and spot what matters without opening anything. Cards left at "None" look exactly as before.

**Automatic ordering.** Within the To Do, Doing and Needs OK columns, cards are now sorted High → Medium → Low → None, and within the same priority they keep their existing oldest-first order. The Done column still sorts by most-recently-finished. Drag-and-drop between columns is unchanged — priority decides the order inside a column, you decide which column.

**Works with the chat assistant too.** The "Add by chat" box now understands priority words. Say "urgent", "asap", "high priority" or "!!" for High; "medium priority" for Medium; "low priority", "whenever" or "someday" for Low (it also accepts P1/P2/P3). The draft card shows the priority chip before you confirm.

**Command line / cross-session support.** `kanban.py` gained priority support so it can be set from any session or script:

- `python3 kanban.py add "Title" --priority high`
- `python3 kanban.py set-priority <id> <high|medium|low|none>`
- `addb64` payloads accept a `"priority"` field.

It accepts common aliases (urgent/p1 → high, med/p2 → medium, lo/p3 → low) and falls back to "none" for anything unrecognised.

## Notes

- **Existing cards** need no migration. Any card without a priority field is treated as "None" — no badge, sorts to the bottom of its column — until you set one.
- I chose priority *levels* with automatic sorting rather than free manual drag-to-reorder within a column. It's less fiddly day to day (you classify once and the board keeps itself ordered) and it reads clearly at a glance. If you'd also like manual hand-ordering inside a column, that's a separate, larger change — say the word and I'll scope it.
