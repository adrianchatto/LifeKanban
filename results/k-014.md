# k-014 — Notion integration: diagnosis and fix

**Status: blocked on you (needs a 2-minute interactive step). Not a code bug.**

## What I found

The Notion connector *is* installed (`plugin:productivity:notion`, pointing at
`https://mcp.notion.com/mcp`). It is not broken — it is simply **not
authenticated**. The server reports: *"installed but requires authentication"*.
That is why it keeps failing: every call needs an OAuth token, and there isn't
one yet. None of Notion's real tools (search, read page, create page, etc.) load
until that handshake is done.

This is the most common cause of a Notion integration that "won't work" — it
looks connected in the plugin list but the OAuth grant was never completed, or
expired/was revoked and needs redoing.

## Why I couldn't finish it in this run

Authorising Notion is interactive: it produces a one-time authorisation URL that
**you** have to open and approve in your browser while signed into the right
Notion workspace. This was an unattended scheduled run, so you weren't here to
click it. Generating the URL now wouldn't help either — those links are
short-lived and would expire before you saw this.

## How to fix it, once and for all (≈2 minutes)

1. Start a normal Cowork chat and say: **"Authenticate Notion"** (or "connect my
   Notion"). I'll trigger the OAuth flow and hand you an authorisation URL.
2. Open that URL in your browser. Sign in to the correct Notion workspace.
3. **Critical step people miss:** on Notion's authorise screen, under
   *"Select pages"*, explicitly grant access to the pages/databases you want
   Claude to reach. If you only grant the top level, sub-pages may stay
   invisible and it'll look "broken" again. Grant the whole workspace, or at
   least every space you actually use.
4. Approve. The browser redirects to a `http://localhost:<port>/callback?...`
   page that may show an error — that's expected on a remote session. Copy the
   **full URL from the address bar** and paste it back to me; I'll complete the
   handshake.
5. Test: I'll run a Notion search to confirm pages come back.

## To make it stick (so it doesn't break "again")

- If it disconnects after a while, the token was revoked or the workspace's admin
  settings expired it — redo steps 1–4; it's the same flow.
- If specific pages 404 but others work, it's the page-permission scope in step
  3, not the connection. Re-run the authorise flow and widen the page selection.
- Make sure you authorise the **work** Notion workspace you actually use, not a
  personal one, if you have more than one signed in.
